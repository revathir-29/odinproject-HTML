Parotta

Description

Parotta is a popular South Indian and Sri Lankan layered flatbread made from maida.
It is known for its flaky, crispy texture achieved through multiple layers of dough folded and shallow-fried until golden brown.

Ingredients

  • Maida
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Oil
  • Water
  • Ghee or Butter
  • Egg
  • Milk

Steps

  1. Mix all-purpose flour, salt, sugar, and oil.
  2. Rest the dough for 30 minutes to improve elasticity.
  3. Divide the dough into balls and flatten them.
  4. Roll tightly into coils and flatten gently.
  5. Roll out each coil into thin circles.
  6. Cook on a hot griddle or tawa with ghee until both sides are golden brown.
  7. Brush with additional ghee or butter during cooking for enhanced flavor and texture.
  8. Serve Parottas hot and fresh.
